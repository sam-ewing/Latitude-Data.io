Latitude and Wind Speed




Plot Summary and Analysis

This scatter plot shows the recorded wind speed for each of the cities sampled based on their latitude value. Cities with a negative latitude value are located in the southern hemisphere, while cities with a positive latitude value are located in the northern hemisphere (latitude 0 representing the equator).

Like the relationship between latitude and temperature, there is also a noticable trend in the severity of wind-speed as latitude approaches the equator. Between -20 and 20 degrees of Latitude, the average wind-speed appears to be between 0 and 10 MPH, while higher windspeeds are recorded as latitude moves toward each pole.

However, it is important to note that the random sample used to generate the graph analyzed in this report appeared to contain significantly more cities in the northern hemisphere than the southern hemisphere. In order to get a more accurate representation of windspeed in the southern hemisphere, I would recommend running the code a few times over to make sure the number of cities in each hemisphere is relatively equal.
