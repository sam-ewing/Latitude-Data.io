Latitude and Maximum Temperature




Plot Summary and Analysis

This scatter plot shows the maximum recorded temperature for each of the cities sampled based on their latitude value. Cities with a negative latitude value are located in the southern hemisphere, while cities with a positive latitude value are located in the northern hemisphere (latitude 0 representing the equator).

This plot shows a clear trend of temperature increasing as latitude approaches the equator. Between -20 and 20 degrees of latitude, temperatures generally tend to average around 80 degrees fahrenheit. Additionally, as latitude moves north (in other words, as latitude increases in a positive manner), the temperature shows a noticable decline, telling us that the weather gets much colder as one moved farther and farther north.

While the same trend can be seen as latitude moves south (in other words, as latitude decreases below 0), the same trend can be seen, although without as much of a dramatic decline in temperature. This can be explained by the earth's alignment relative to the sun. Temperatures in these areas of the world may be higher since the southern hemisphere is closer to the sun at the time of this analysis (November 11, 2019), and is preparing to enter their summer season, while the northern hemisphere is farther from the sun at the time of this report and is preparing to enter their winter season.

However, it is important to note that the graph analyzed this report seemed to display a higher porportion of cities in the northern hemisphere than cities in the southern hemisphere. In order to confirm this analysis, I would recommend running the code a few times over to make sure the number of cities in each hemisphere is relatively equal.
